Total Library Attendance Throughout the Years
Public libraries are a critical element of free education and community. Technology has made information more accessible, influencing library attendance.

Library attendance has gradually declined from 2013-2020 and plummeted due to Covid-19. As the pandemic has ended, more people are returning to Chicago public libraries, but in far fewer numbers.

Chicago Public Libraries Maps
These maps show the distribution of library attendees in Chicago in the years 2013 and 2023. The darker the section, the more attendees to libraries in that zip code. The dots signify the location of the individual libraries Search up a library to see how many visitors it had in that year.

2013 Chicago Public Libraries

A map of Chicago's 80 libraries divided by zip codes.

The zip code 60605 overwhelmingly has the most visitors. This is because of the historic Harold Washington Library, which garners the most visitors.

2023 Chicago Public Libraries

Two libraries have been added in the past 10 years: Little Italy (2019) and Water Works (2021).

Harold Washington Library still has the most visitors. However, there is a more even distribution of library visitors across Chicago compared to 2013.

Though the attendance of libraries has evened out in Chicago, it is important to remember that every library has seen a drop of attendance. Below is a side-by-side comparison of monthly visitors in 2013 and 2023
